Berwick sits in one of the leafier parts of SE Melbourne. The streets around Berwick Village, the Outlook Estate and the established residential pockets off Clyde Road have mature eucalypt and native tree coverage that makes gutter maintenance a genuine priority, not a once-every-few-years task.

## Why Berwick Gutters Fill Faster

Eucalypts drop year-round. Unlike deciduous trees that shed in a single autumn event, gum trees shed bark strips, seed pods and leaves across all four seasons. This means Berwick gutters accumulate debris steadily rather than in one seasonal burst.

Autumn brings the heaviest load. The period from April through June sees significant leaf fall and the first heavy rain events. Gutters carrying a full season of debris are at their most vulnerable when Melbourne's autumn rain arrives. Debris that was dry and loose becomes wet and compacted, blocking downpipes quickly.

Spring adds a second risk period. The storms that move through SE Melbourne from September to November push additional debris off trees and into gutters that may still be carrying material from winter.

## What to Watch for in Berwick's Older Housing Stock

Much of Berwick's established housing dates from the 1980s and 1990s. Guttering from this period is reaching the age where rust, sagging joins and failed sealant points start to appear. Regular cleaning reduces the stress on ageing guttering, but it also creates the opportunity to identify sections that need repair before they fail.

Properties on Berwick's sloping terrain, around the Outlook Estate particularly, often have longer roof runs and valley sections that concentrate debris. These areas block first and overflow first if not maintained.

## How Often

Most Berwick properties benefit from twice-yearly cleaning. Late autumn and early spring cover the two main risk windows. Properties sitting directly under large gums, common throughout the older parts of the suburb, may need attention every four to five months.
